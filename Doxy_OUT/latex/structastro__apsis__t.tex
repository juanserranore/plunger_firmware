\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
An apsis event\+: pericenter (closest approach) or apocenter (farthest distance). 

For the Moon orbiting the Earth, or a planet orbiting the Sun, an {\itshape apsis} is an event where the orbiting body reaches its closest or farthest point from the primary body. The closest approach is called {\itshape pericenter} and the farthest point is {\itshape apocenter}.

More specific terminology is common for particular orbiting bodies. The Moon\textquotesingle{}s closest approach to the Earth is called {\itshape perigee} and its farthest point is called {\itshape apogee}. The closest approach of a planet to the Sun is called {\itshape perihelion} and the furthest point is called {\itshape aphelion}.

This data structure is returned by Astronomy\+\_\+\+Search\+Lunar\+Apsis and Astronomy\+\_\+\+Next\+Lunar\+Apsis to iterate through consecutive alternating perigees and apogees.
