\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Information about a solar eclipse as seen by an observer at a given time and geographic location. 

Returned by Astronomy\+\_\+\+Search\+Local\+Solar\+Eclipse or Astronomy\+\_\+\+Next\+Local\+Solar\+Eclipse to report information about a solar eclipse as seen at a given geographic location. If a solar eclipse is found, {\ttfamily status} holds {\ttfamily ASTRO\+\_\+\+SUCCESS} and the other fields are set. If {\ttfamily status} holds any other value, it is an error code and the other fields are undefined.

When a solar eclipse is found, it is classified as partial, annular, or total. The {\ttfamily kind} field thus holds {\ttfamily ECLIPSE\+\_\+\+PARTIAL}, {\ttfamily ECLIPSE\+\_\+\+ANNULAR}, or {\ttfamily ECLIPSE\+\_\+\+TOTAL}. A partial solar eclipse is when the Moon does not line up directly enough with the Sun to completely block the Sun\textquotesingle{}s light from reaching the observer. An annular eclipse occurs when the Moon\textquotesingle{}s disc is completely visible against the Sun but the Moon is too far away to completely block the Sun\textquotesingle{}s light; this leaves the Sun with a ring-\/like appearance. A total eclipse occurs when the Moon is close enough to the Earth and aligned with the Sun just right to completely block all sunlight from reaching the observer.

There are 5 "{}event"{} fields, each of which contains a time and a solar altitude. Field {\ttfamily peak} holds the date and time of the center of the eclipse, when it is at its peak. The fields {\ttfamily partial\+\_\+begin} and {\ttfamily partial\+\_\+end} are always set, and indicate when the eclipse begins/ends. If the eclipse reaches totality or becomes annular, {\ttfamily total\+\_\+begin} and {\ttfamily total\+\_\+end} indicate when the total/annular phase begins/ends. When an event field is valid, the caller must also check its {\ttfamily altitude} field to see whether the Sun is above the horizon at that time. See astro\+\_\+eclipse\+\_\+kind\+\_\+t for more information.
