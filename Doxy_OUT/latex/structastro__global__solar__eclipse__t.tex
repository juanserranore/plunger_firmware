\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Reports the time and geographic location of the peak of a solar eclipse. 

Returned by Astronomy\+\_\+\+Search\+Global\+Solar\+Eclipse or Astronomy\+\_\+\+Next\+Global\+Solar\+Eclipse to report information about a solar eclipse event. If a solar eclipse is found, {\ttfamily status} holds {\ttfamily ASTRO\+\_\+\+SUCCESS} and {\ttfamily kind}, {\ttfamily peak}, and {\ttfamily distance} have valid values. The {\ttfamily latitude} and {\ttfamily longitude} are set only for total and annular eclipses (see more below). If {\ttfamily status} holds any value other than {\ttfamily ASTRO\+\_\+\+SUCCESS}, it is an error code; in that case, {\ttfamily kind} holds {\ttfamily ECLIPSE\+\_\+\+NONE} and all the other fields are undefined.

Field {\ttfamily peak} holds the date and time of the peak of the eclipse, defined as the instant when the axis of the Moon\textquotesingle{}s shadow cone passes closest to the Earth\textquotesingle{}s center.

The eclipse is classified as partial, annular, or total, depending on the maximum amount of the Sun\textquotesingle{}s disc obscured, as seen at the peak location on the surface of the Earth.

The {\ttfamily kind} field thus holds {\ttfamily ECLIPSE\+\_\+\+PARTIAL}, {\ttfamily ECLIPSE\+\_\+\+ANNULAR}, or {\ttfamily ECLIPSE\+\_\+\+TOTAL}. A total eclipse is when the peak observer sees the Sun completely blocked by the Moon. An annular eclipse is like a total eclipse, but the Moon is too far from the Earth\textquotesingle{}s surface to completely block the Sun; instead, the Sun takes on a ring-\/shaped appearance. A partial eclipse is when the Moon blocks part of the Sun\textquotesingle{}s disc, but nobody on the Earth observes either a total or annular eclipse.

If {\ttfamily kind} is {\ttfamily ECLIPSE\+\_\+\+TOTAL} or {\ttfamily ECLIPSE\+\_\+\+ANNULAR}, the {\ttfamily latitude} and {\ttfamily longitude} fields give the geographic coordinates of the center of the Moon\textquotesingle{}s shadow projected onto the daytime side of the Earth at the instant of the eclipse\textquotesingle{}s peak. If {\ttfamily kind} has any other value, {\ttfamily latitude} and {\ttfamily longitude} are undefined and should not be used.
